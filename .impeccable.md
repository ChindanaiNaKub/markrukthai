## Design Context

### Users
Makruk (Thai Chess) enthusiasts, casual players, and learners. They likely play on both desktop and mobile. They want a fast, reliable, and aesthetically pleasing platform to play a traditional game in a modern context.

### Brand Personality
- **Traditional yet Modern**: Respects the heritage of Makruk while offering a contemporary web experience.
- **Warm & Sophisticated**: Uses a palette inspired by natural Thai materials (wood, silk, amber) rather than cold tech grays.
- **Reliable & Focused**: A "zero-distraction" environment for deep play, similar to the Lichess philosophy.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual Tone**: Refined, warm-tinted dark mode with wooden board textures. High-quality SVG pieces that evoke traditional craftsmanship.
- **References**: Lichess (for layout and UX philosophy), traditional Thai architecture (for color inspiration).
- **Theme**: Dark mode by default, using warm OKLCH colors. No pure blacks or grays.

### Design Principles
1. **Warmth over Gray**: All neutrals must be tinted with a subtle warm hue (amber/wood).
2. **Clarity of Play**: The board and pieces are the primary focus. UI chrome should be supportive, not competitive.
3. **Intentional Motion**: Use subtle, high-quality transitions (ease-out-quart/expo) for piece movement and UI reveals. Avoid "bouncy" animations.
4. **Cultural Resonance**: Subtle nods to Thai heritage through typography (Noto Sans Thai) and color, without falling into stereotypes.
5. **Fast & Responsive**: The interface must feel immediate and adapt seamlessly to different screen sizes without losing functionality.
